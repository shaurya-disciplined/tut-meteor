# 01 · Purpose and Audience

## What this is

`tut::meteor` is a personal site. Not a portfolio in the resume sense, not a landing page selling anything.
It is the one link Shaurya drops in his Discord profile so that when he meets someone new in a large
"Study Together" server, they can click it and understand his vibe without him having to explain himself
again. The handle is `tut.meteor`. The alias is **Meteor**.

## Who visits

Usually a stranger from Discord. Often another student. Curious, scrolling on a phone, deciding in a few
seconds whether this person is interesting. The site is built for that moment.

## The core intent

**Intrigue, not inform.** The visitor should leave with a mood and just enough detail to be curious, never a
full data sheet. The energy of every page is "there is more, and you do not get all of it here." This is the
opposite of a LinkedIn profile. Where LinkedIn is open and founder forward, the site is reserved and withholding.

## Hard constraints and permissions

- **Local only.** The site is never deployed, never published, never commercial. It runs on the owner's
  machine and gets shared as needed.
- **Because it is local and personal, any image or video from anywhere on the internet, or AI generated, may
  be used freely.** There are no licensing concerns for this project. Source or generate whatever serves the
  visuals.
- **Alias forward.** The public face is "Meteor / tut.meteor." The real name appears only in the `<head>`
  metadata. Age (17) and height are deliberately kept off the site.
- **Phones are the primary audience.** Most visitors arrive from a Discord link on mobile. Everything must
  feel right on a mid range phone first.

## What the previous version got wrong (so we never regress)

The site before NOCTURNE was too simple and too revealing. It bobbed everything forever, used emojis and
neon glow and a generic font, and it overshared with motivational declarations, personal stats, and
"documenting my journey publicly." NOCTURNE inverts all of that: triggered motion instead of perpetual
bobbing, a couture serif instead of a generic sans, one restrained metallic accent instead of neon, and
reserved implication instead of confession.
